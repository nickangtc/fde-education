# God I love mondays

_Slow Riches · Nick Ang · March 31, 2026_  
_The one thing that turned my relationship with the workweek completely upside down_

Source: <https://nickang.substack.com/p/god-i-love-mondays>

When you work for yourself, when you get to dictate what you focus on, where to pour your heart, who to ignore and who to engage, life changes completely.

Like any employee, I used to dread Mondays.

Mondays represented the start of a week-long slog. It meant being answerable to your boss and your boss's boss, doing what they deemed fit. My time clearly wasn't mine.

Quitting and going solo totally transformed my relationship with weekdays.

Now when Sunday night comes around, I'm actually happy, not sad. When I put Charlotte to bed, I'd start literally fantasising about what I'd do on Monday. Recently that fantasy went to many corners:

- I'll record 3 reels to grow my Instagram following.
- I'll squeeze in a Substack post to share updates with my more devoted audience.
- I'll test a hypothesis: start each reel with a talking-head hook rather than gesturing.
- I'll look through Friday's 3 reels to analyse performance and extract new hypotheses.
- I'd like to add in-app notifications to Album so I can see when friends put a new post up.
- For lunch I want to try that new Chinese place that my wife Charlane cycled past yesterday.

I wrote a really detailed list not to show the exact thought process, but to show the powerful drive that underlies everything. There's curiosity, passion, interest, and energy. After quitting my job and going solo, that drive has grown and waned, but it's trending generally upwards. I'd even describe it sometimes as exhilarating.

The other day, our friends were over for dinner. One of them said, "So, Charlotte has kindergarten tomorrow, right?" I thought for a while and replied, "No, tomorrow is Sunday, right?"

My friends gave me a puzzled look. I returned the same look, because I genuinely thought it was Saturday. Turns out it was already Sunday. I realised this and blurted out, "Omg! Tomorrow's Monday? Yay!" Of course, I had to explain myself to my friends, who are still employees.

When I first realised that I'd become such a person, I was incredibly happy and in disbelief. I never knew that I could feel so much empowerment and so much joy from working. But it's true. It's here. This is now my reality, five days a week.

I've become the person who can't wait for Monday. And I'm never going back.
