Alright, I've got about an hour to write this newsletter, so let's dive right into it.

In this post I'll share what happened and what I learned from growing a new Instagram account from 0 followers to 327 followers in one week.

The results surprised me: the uneven distribution of attention, the two viral videos, and the effects on my brain.

## Reflections about virality

- In an [earlier post](https://nickang.substack.com/p/when-success-comes-fast-you-know), I said I was aiming for 500 followers by 8 April. That goal is within reach if one of my reels goes viral again.
- For context, I gave myself one month to get to 500 followers by using my wife Charlane's playbook. She's grown her account from 0 to almost 7000 followers in less than two months.
- Did I give up? Yes. Shortly after starting the account and posting my first 5 reels, I felt disgust towards what I was doing. I realised from that that I can't simply copy someone's playbook exactly. I almost gave up entirely and never came back.
- So from 10 March to 27 March, I basically posted nothing because I'd given up. Yet I'm still close to my 500-followers target. This surprises me.
- I gave Instagram another shot because of two reasons: Charlane's explosive growth showed me it can be rewarding for everyone involved, and I remembered Zander Whitehurst's Instagram account and believed I could do something similar.
- The playbook has one core tenet: copy and paste.
- Copying what's working can move you much faster than insisting on originality too early.
- What copy and paste means: find someone whose style you can emulate and copy their style and format while bringing your own content.
- It can also mean bookmarking 100 reels that made you stop and feel something, then copying the exact format: visual hook, caption shape, duration, even music choice.
- Once I started copy and pasting, I realised how many reels are repeated. And there seems to be no cap to the engagement on each duplicated idea because either people forget or plenty more people haven't seen that concept yet.
- Charlane repeated one reel exactly and exceeded the view count of the original.
- Even when you try to make an exact copy, you still end up with something unique because your face, timing, body language, lighting, location, and personality come through.
- [Everything is a remix](https://www.everythingisaremix.info/), not a carbon copy.
- I now know I'll never know which video is original and which is a copy. Highest view count does not prove originality.
- Does it matter who did it first? This is not academia. Instagram is a meme distribution machine.
- Memes are surprisingly effective in getting culturally relevant ideas to spread across the internet and society.
- Memes are also compact. Working on Instagram has taught me more about packaging ideas than almost any other endeavour.
- I have to first make interesting content before the useful part of the content gets an audience.
- I've heard Nicolas Cole say that if you want to earn money through writing, you should not start a blog. Now I understand that emotionally, not just intellectually.
- "Don't start a blog" means don't publish where there is no built-in distribution.
- Virality is how you go from nobody hearing what you say to at least some people hearing it within a timeframe that is feasible for bootstrapped solopreneurs.
- Instagram and TikTok are massively viral networks. Substack is not really viral; it is more diffusion than virality.
- LinkedIn is somewhat viral, but loses points because the network is much smaller than Instagram's.

I hope that by reading this post, you take away the idea that "viral" is not a dirty word, copying is really remixing, and that the current form of social media *can* have some positive impact in this world if you use it well.

I intend to continue spending time producing reels on my account. Updated goal for the end of April: 5000 followers. It's extremely ambitious, but since I'm outperforming Charlane's own early growth when comparing initial spurts, I think I have a tiny chance at it.

Ultimately, as a solopreneur, follower count means distribution, and distribution is one of the keys to unlocking sales.

The nice thing is that by making reels, I'm forced to learn about my interests and how to package them in ways that have the highest chance of being well received. I'm effectively already doing sales.
